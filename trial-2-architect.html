THE PROPOSAL

CAMPANHÃ GREEN TECHNOLOGY HUB
URBAN REGENERATION PROPOSAL
SUBMITTED BY: SENIOR URBAN PLANNER  |  LOCATION: CAMPANHÃ, PORTO  |  SITE: 2.4 HA BROWNFIELD  |  WORD COUNT: ~295

The 2.4-hectare brownfield site adjacent to the Campanhã rail terminal presents a rare opportunity to transform post-industrial land into a productive mixed-use district. The proposed Green Technology Hub would repurpose an under-used corridor connecting the eastern residential quarters to the city centre, addressing documented gaps in local employment, green infrastructure, and technical workspace capacity.

The architectural programme centres on a cluster of low-rise, high-performance laboratory and office buildings arranged around a 0.6-hectare publicly accessible courtyard. Building envelopes will incorporate triple-glazed, high-albedo facades combined with deep overhangs calibrated to achieve a 35% reduction in solar heat gain relative to current stock in the area — eliminating the need for mechanical cooling during seven months of the year. Photovoltaic arrays across all roof surfaces and south-facing cladding panels are sized to supply 60% of common-area electricity demand from day one of operation.

A closed-loop greywater system and permeable paving throughout will achieve a 40% reduction in potable water consumption compared with standard commercial construction. The courtyard's biodiverse planting plan — selected in consultation with the Porto Botanic Garden — provides measurable cooling, stormwater attenuation, and recreational amenity without ornamental excess.

Economic projections, independently verified, indicate 340 direct positions within 36 months of opening — spanning laboratory technicians, logistics, facilities management, and knowledge-economy roles — alongside 120 indirect jobs in supply-chain and service sectors. Three anchor tenants from the life sciences and clean-energy instrumentation sectors have submitted letters of intent pending planning approval.

This proposal asks the Council to designate the site for mixed-use technology and public-realm use, issue an Expressions of Interest notice to anchor tenants, and commission a full Environmental Impact Assessment within 60 days.

## ANTI-CLICHÉ BRAND FILTER
## Version 1.0 — Invicta-One Phase 1, Trial 2

### PURPOSE
Prevent the use of overused corporate buzzwords. Force the AI
to express concepts with concrete, specific, measurable language.

### BANNED WORD LIST (expandable)
The following words are FORBIDDEN in all output.
When you encounter a concept that would normally use these words,
replace it with specific, tangible language:

| Banned Word  | Replace With                                          |
|--------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| smart        | describe the specific mechanism (e.g., "sensor-based", "automated") |
| sustainable  | cite the specific metric (e.g., "60% lower energy use") |
| innovative   | describe what makes it different (e.g., "first municipal deployment") |
| synergy      | describe the shared mechanism (e.g., "shared heat exchange loop") |
| future       | use a specific timeframe (e.g., "within 18 months")   |
| leverage     | use (e.g., "use", "apply", "deploy")                  |
| ecosystem    | describe the specific network (e.g., "supply chain of 12 vendors") |
| paradigm     | describe the shift concretely                         |
| disruptive   | cite the specific displacement or improvement         |
| scalable     | cite the growth capacity in numbers                   |

### ENFORCEMENT RULES
1. SELF-AUDIT: Before finalising any output, scan for banned words.
   If found, rewrite the sentence using the replacement guidance above.
2. SPECIFICITY MANDATE: Every claim must be backed by a number, name,
   timeframe, or mechanism. No vague assertions allowed.
3. PERSONA FIDELITY: When a persona is assigned (e.g., "Senior Urban
   Planner"), all language, tone, and assumptions must match that
   professional's vocabulary and concerns — not a marketing writer's.

### HOW TO USE
Add this block to the system prompt of any content-generation,
proposal-writing, or communications task. Extend the banned word
list as needed for your brand or domain.
